What is Childhood Cancer?

Childhood cancer, also known as pediatric cancer, refers to cancer that occurs in children and adolescents. It is a broad term that encompasses various types of cancer that can develop in individuals below the age of 18. Just like cancer in adults, childhood cancer involves the abnormal growth and division of cells that form tumors or affect the blood and bone marrow.

Various types of cancer can affect children, including leukemia (cancer of the blood), brain and central nervous system tumors, neuroblastoma (a cancer that develops in immature nerve cells), Wilms tumor (a kidney cancer), retinoblastoma (a cancer of the eye), and others. The specific type of cancer that develops in children can vary based on age, genetics, and other factors.
