What are the differences cancers in Adults and Children?

Adults

  • Types of cancers: Common types include lung cancer, colorectal cancer, breast cancer, prostate cancer, pancreatic cancer, and more.
  • Cancer incidence: The risk of developing cancer increases with age.
  • Treatment approaches: Adults typically undergo treatments such as surgery, radiation therapy, chemotherapy, targeted therapy, immunotherapy, and hormone therapy, depending on the specific type and stage of cancer.
  • Prognosis: The prognosis for adult cancers varies widely depending on the type and stage of cancer. Advances in treatment have improved survival rates for many types of adult cancers.

Childhood

  • Types of cancers: Common types in children include leukemia (blood cancer), brain tumors, neuroblastoma, Wilms tumor (kidney cancer), and certain types of sarcomas.
  • Cancer incidence: Childhood cancer is relatively rare compared to adult cancer but remains a significant health concern. It is the leading cause of death by disease in children.
  • Treatment approaches: Pediatric cancers are treated with a combination of surgery, radiation therapy, chemotherapy, and sometimes stem cell transplantation. Treatment plans prioritize minimizing long-term side effects and preserving overall development and growth.
  • Prognosis: Childhood cancers generally have a higher overall survival rate compared to some adult cancers, with many types being highly treatable.
