Childhood Cancer Types: We aim to provide you with a deeper understanding of the various types of cancer that can affect children. By equipping parents, caregivers, and healthcare professionals with valuable knowledge about these cancer types, we hope to empower them in their journey of supporting and caring for children with cancer.

A brain tumor refers to an abnormal growth of cells within the brain or the central nervous system. It can develop in various parts of the brain, including the brain tissue, membranes surrounding the brain, cranial nerves, or the pituitary or pineal glands. Brain tumors can be either benign or malignant.

Neuroblastoma is a type of cancer that originates from immature nerve cells, called neuroblasts, which are found in various parts of the body. It most commonly affects young children, usually under the age of 5, and it accounts for a significant portion of pediatric cancer cases.

Lymphomas are a type of cancer that affect the lymphatic system, which is a part of the body's immune system. The lymphatic system includes lymph nodes, lymphatic vessels, and organs such as the spleen and thymus.

A spinal cord tumor is a growth or mass that develops within or near the spinal cord or the surrounding structures. These tumors can be benign or malignant and can originate from the spinal cord tissue itself or from adjacent tissues such as the meninges, nerve roots, or vertebral bones.

A kidney tumor refers to the development of abnormal growth or mass within the kidney. The tumor can be benign (non-cancerous) or malignant (cancerous), and it can arise from different types of cells within the kidney.

A malignant bone tumor refers to a cancerous growth that originates in the bone tissue. These tumors can be primary, meaning they start within the bone itself, or secondary/metastatic, meaning they spread to the bone from cancer that originated in another part of the body.
